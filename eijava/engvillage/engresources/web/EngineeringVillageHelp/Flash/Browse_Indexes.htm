Browse Indexes

The Expert and Quick Search pages provide access to various indexes that you can use in your searches. As shown below, the Browse Indexes box has a list of several indexes to choose from:

  1. Click an index in the list to display a pop-up with many choices.

Note: The indexes listed in the Browse Indexes box depend on the database(s) you've selected. In Quick Search, when some databases are selected, the Browse Indexes box does not appear. Also, if you have selected more than one database, the database name is indicated after the term.

  1. After the index loads, search for a specific term, or click a letter A through Z to open a list of terms to browse. In the example below, the user searched for electron.

  1. Click the AND or OR connect terms, if desired.

  2. Click to check the index(es) you want to use in your search. Keep the following tips in mind:

    • In Expert Search, when you select a term from the index, it is automatically pasted into the search box with the wn (within) command and the field code. You can select as many terms as needed.

    • In Quick Search, the SEARCH IN box is changed to the appropriate field.

    • Deselecting the term from the index removes it from the search box.

    • If you select more than three terms, the fourth term overwrites the term in the third search box.

The Author Browse Index is useful for searching on author names, especially for searching for authors with very common surnames. For example, searching on author Alan Smith, as Smith, A* retrieves records by other authors such as Smith, Albert or Smith, Alex.

Ei Patents Indexes

When searching the patents databases alone, two indexes are available:

Inventor - This field contains the inventor(s) names of patented item. You can search the index for a specific inventor by entering the first letters of the name and clicking Find. Next to each name, the patent database is indicated in brackets (US or European patent).

[Patent] Assignee - This field contains the name of the individual or entity to which ownership of the patent was assigned at the time of patent issue. You can search the index for a specific assignee by entering the first letters of the name and clicking Find. Next to each name, the patent database is indicated in brackets (US or European patent).
