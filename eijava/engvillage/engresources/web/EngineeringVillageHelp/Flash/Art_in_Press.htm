Articles in Press

Articles in Press (AiPs) are documents in Compendex that have been accepted for publication but have not yet, in most cases, been assigned to a journal issue. It is an article that is still in progress. Having access to Articles in Press allows you to obtain critical and current information more quickly.

The Article in Press designation (shown below) appears directly above the Compendex database notation in Quick Search and Expert Search results.

The designation also appears in the Detailed and Abstract displays. In the detailed display, an Article in Press record displays a temporary accession number in alphanumeric format, such as IP12345678.

The accession number is formatted as follows:

  • First four digits = the production year

  • Next two digits = the production week

    •  For example, in the accession number 20103613210323, 2010 = production year, 36 = production week

  • Remaining 8 digits = no meaning

See Compendex and Engineering Index Backfile Search Fields for information about the accession numbers before 1970.

When the Article in Press is replaced in the future with the final version of the record, it will display a permanent Compendex accession number, such as 20100123456789. The icon and Article in Press designation also disappear.

Three types of Articles in Press are available in the Compendex database:

Accepted manuscripts

Accepted manuscripts are articles that have been peer-reviewed and accepted for publication by the Editorial Board. These articles have not yet been copyedited and/or formatted in the journal house style.

Uncorrected proofs

Uncorrected proofs have been copyedited and formatted but have not been finalized and may still be corrected by the authors, so the text could change before final publication.

Corrected proofs

Corrected proofs contain authors' corrections and may or may not have specific issue and page numbers assigned.
