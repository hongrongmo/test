Referex Search Fields

The following fields are searchable in Referex:

  • Keyword

  • Author

  • ISBN

  • Title

  • Publisher

  • Subject

 

Keyword

Keyword is the default search field for Referex. Searching by keyword locates terms within the text of the eBook. Results are returned by relevance. The relevance sort is based on an algorithm that takes into account the following:

 

  • Whether the words are found as an exact phrase or separately;

  • When words are found separately, closer proximity ranks higher;

  • The number of times that the word/phrase appears in the section of an eBook;

  • The word's location within the section (words found at the beginning of the section rank higher than words found towards the end); and

  • How often the word appears in the database as a whole (words used often are less relevant than words that are less common)

 

To search for an exact phrase, enclose term within braces or quotation marks:

 

Example: "solar energy"; {avalanche diodes}

 

Author

Referex cites author, editor or contributors' names as they appear in the eBook.

 

Surnames appear first, followed by a comma and the remainder of the name as it appears in the publication. Editors and contributors are also listed in the author field.

 

Author names can be truncated by using an asterisk (*) as the truncation symbol:

 

Example: carpenter, p*

 

When records appear, author names are hyperlinked. Click on an author hyperlink to retrieve additional eBooks by that author from the Referex database.

 

ISBN (International Standard Book Number)

ISBN are ten-character alphanumeric representations of book titles. The ISBN can be used to limit results to a specific book. You can find a book's ISBN at the citation and Book Description level of a record:

 

Example: 0884156516

 

Title

If you want to search for specific terms within the title of the eBook, search on the Title field:

 

Example: "air cushion craft"; {petrochemical plant}

 

Publisher

Search on the "Publisher" field to identify publishers, or find the eBooks published by a particular publisher.

 

Note: If you want to limit a search to a publisher name fragment (e.g. Elsevier instead of Elsevier Newnes or Elsevier Academic Press) enclose it in quotations or brackets:

 

Example: "elsevier"; {elsevier}

 

Subject

Referex contains controlled vocabulary, which is represented as a list of subject terms used to describe the content of an eBook in the most specific and consistent way possible. You can see them in the Browse eBooks section of the eBook search.

 

You can also search for controlled vocabulary by entering a term and selecting Subject from the "Search In" pull-down menu.

 

Subject terms appear hyperlinked in the Book Description format. By clicking on a subject term, this will retrieve additional records from within the collection(s) included in the original search.

 

Example: clicking on Aircraft design in a book description record retrieves more books on this subject.
