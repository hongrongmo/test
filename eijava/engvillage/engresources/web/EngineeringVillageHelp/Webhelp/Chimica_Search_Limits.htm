Chimica Search Fields and Limits

The following fields are searchable in Chimica:

Click here for a full list of all available search fields on Expert search.

All fields

All fields will retrieve results from any of the following fields: title, author, corresponding author's affiliation, source title, CODEN, ISSN, language, controlled and uncontrolled terms, EMTREE Classification Code, CAS Registry Number, and abstract.

"All Fields" is the default for Chimica.

Subject/Title/Abstract

"Subject/Title/Abstract" will retrieve results from any of the following fields: title, controlled and uncontrolled terms, EMTREE Classification Code, CAS Registry Number, and abstract.

Author

Since author names may be cited in a variety of formats, use of the Author Browse Index is highly recommended. Author names can be truncated by using an asterisk (*) as the truncation symbol:

Smith A* will retrieve Smith A., Smith A. C. Smith Agnes etc.

Be aware that this strategy may lead to false hits because there are many authors with the same last name and first initial.

When records are displayed, author names are hyperlinked. Click on an author hyperlink to retrieve records by that author from the entire date range of the database.

Author affiliation

The formats and abbreviations used in author affiliations have changed over the years. Use of the author affiliation Browse Index is highly recommended. Author affiliations are displayed in the detailed record.

Source title

Search on the "Source Title" field if you want to find articles from a particular journal.

Title

If you want to search for specific terms within the title, search on the "Title" field. Words in a title are often indicators of the main focus of the article.

"Chlorophyll" in the title will likely find articles where "Chlorophyll" is an important aspect of the article.

Index Term

The controlled vocabulary used to index records in Chimica can be found in the EMBASE Thesaurus.

These terms appear hyperlinked in the abstract and detailed record format. Clicking on any of these will retrieve records spanning the default time period of the database.

Limiting Your Search

Language You can limit your search to any language listed in the drop-down menu: All languages, English, Chinese, French, German, Japanese, Russian or Spanish.

To search for more than one language, or to search for a language not listed in the Quick Search pull-down menu, use Expert Search. A complete list of available languages within the Chimica database is in the Language Browse Index in Expert Search.

The language of the document is given at the end of the citation if it is other than English.

If more than one language is given, they are separated by a comma, for example:

French, German

Limit by Date

Year Limits

If you do not find what you need, broaden your search by selecting the date range limit and expanding the range of your search to the last year, the last five years, etc.

Updates

Selecting this option allows you to limit your search by the 1, 2, 3, or 4 most recent database updates. Chimica is updated weekly.

Browse indexes

From the Browse Indexes box (located on the right hand side of the page), select the index you wish to use by clicking on the radio button next to it and then clicking on "Browse."

Once the index is loaded, you can navigate by either selecting the first letter(s) of the term you wish to search for or by typing in the first few letters of the term in the SEARCH FOR box and clicking on "Find."

In Quick Search, when you select a term from the index, it is automatically pasted into the first available textbox. The SEARCH IN box will also be changed to the appropriate field. Un-selecting the term from the index will remove it from the textbox. If you select more than three terms, the fourth term will overwrite the term in the third textbox.

You can also choose to have the second and third index terms pasted from the indexes with either a Boolean AND or OR connecting them.  For more information, refer to the Boolean Operators section.

The Author Browse Index is useful for searching on author names, especially for searching for authors with very common surnames. For example:

Searching on author Alan Smith, as Smith, A* will retrieve Smith A., Smith A.C., Smith A. E., Smith Allen, Smith Andrew T., etc.
