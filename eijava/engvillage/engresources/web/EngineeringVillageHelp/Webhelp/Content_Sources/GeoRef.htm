GeoRef

GeoRef is a database of abstracted and indexed geosciences literature published by the American Geological Institute (AGI). GeoRef provides a comprehensive history of geology and its subfields with expertly indexed records from over 3,500 scholarly journals, books, maps, and conference proceedings. In addition, the database includes all publications of the US Geological Survey and masters’ theses and dissertations from United States and Canadian universities.

GeoRef gives researchers access to the information they need to efficiently evaluate geology and its role in the context of history, economics, engineering, and more. Unique to Engineering Village, GeoRef offers a new results-based mapping tool that plots geographical features from a result set onto a digital map, providing both context and insight into search results.

GeoRef contains over 2.9 million abstract records over 200 years of geological literature from North America (1785-present), plus 75 years of geological literature from the rest of the world (1933-present). GeoRef's accurate, relevant records are indexed specifically for geosciences research using controlled vocabulary from the GeoRef Thesaurus. GeoRef is updated weekly and there are 90.000 new records that are added annually.
