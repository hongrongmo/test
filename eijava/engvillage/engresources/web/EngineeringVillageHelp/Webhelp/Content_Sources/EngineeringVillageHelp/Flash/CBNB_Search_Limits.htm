CBNB Search Fields and Limits

The following fields are searchable in CBNB:

Click here for a full list of all available search fields on Expert search

All fields

"All Fields" will retrieve results from any of the following fields: title, source title, document type, scope, CODEN, ISSN, language, company name, CAS Registry Number, industrial sector and sector code, and abstract.

Subject/Title/Abstract

"Subject/Title/Abstract" will retrieve results from any of the following fields: title, company name, CAS Registry Number, industrial sector and sector code, and abstract.

Company

All companies for which information is given in the abstract are indexed. The legal designations of the status of a company are not used in indexing, e.g. "ICI" is used rather than "ICI PLC".

Subsidiaries of multinationals are separately named. The parent company will be indexed if it is mentioned in the abstract, but there is no automatic linking of subsidiaries and parents.

Full names are not reversed, but initials in company names are reversed, e.g. "FH Faulding" is indexed as "Faulding, FH."

An 'e' is inserted where a German umlaut occurs, e.g. "Ruetgerswerke."

Acronyms are given when they are commonly used, e.g. "Mines de Potasse d'Alsace" can also be searched as "MDPA."

Title

Title searches for words that appear in the title (or English translation title) of the article. This is very useful if you are looking for articles on a very broad topic. Words in a title are often an indication of the importance of those terms to the article.

Source title

Select "Source Title" if you want to find articles from a specific journal.

Limit by Date

Year Limits

If you do not find what you need, broaden your search by selecting the date range limit and expanding the range of your search to the last year, the last five years, etc.

Updates

Selecting this option allows you to limit your search by the 1, 2, 3, or 4 most recent database updates. CBNB is updated weekly.

Browse indexes

From the Browse Indexes box (located on the right side of the page), select the index you wish to use by clicking on the radio button next to it and then clicking on "Browse."

Once the index is loaded, you can navigate by either selecting the first letter(s) of the term you wish to search for or by typing in the first few letters of the term in the SEARCH FOR box and clicking on "Find."

In Quick Search, when you select a term from the index, it is automatically pasted into the first available textbox. The SEARCH IN box will also be changed to the appropriate field. Un-selecting the term from the index will remove it from the textbox. If you select more than three terms, the fourth term will overwrite the term in the third textbox.

You can also choose to have the second and third index terms pasted from the indexes with either a Boolean AND or OR connecting them.  For more information, refer to the Boolean Operators section.
